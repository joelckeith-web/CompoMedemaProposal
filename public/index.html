Four Deliverables That Change Everything
Both plans center on the same core question: how do all your touchpoints work together to move someone from stranger to raving fan?
01
Customer Journey Map
Visual flowchart mapping where each of your 30+ touchpoints sits across 7 stages — from someone who's never heard of you to a raving fan who refers everyone they know. Shows the gaps: where do people fall out between an offline interaction and an online discovery? Where is there no digital bridge?
02
Ideal Customer Profiles (x2)
Two detailed personas (e.g., first-time buyer in Farmington, downsizing senior). Trigger events that start their search, where they hang out, how they find agents, what makes them choose one over another. This shapes every piece of content you create and where you distribute it.
03
Content Distribution System
One-page flowchart showing how a single piece of content moves across all platforms. Podcast episode → clips → social posts → GBP update → email mention → magazine tie-in. Who's responsible for each step (Livvia, Madison, Mitchell Logic). Weekly and monthly cadence. Cuts time and amplifies reach.
04
Brand Architecture Framework
How Opening Doors, The Local Loop, Holly Days, CARES, the magazine, annettecompo.com, and the Compo & Medema Group RE brand all connect under one umbrella. Not a redesign — a hierarchy that makes every sub-brand reinforce the parent. One story, different arms.

PLAN A — CORE
Strategy First: The Session
One 60–90 minute led conversation. Four focused blocks covering every angle of your digital brand.

Block 1 — ICP Development (20 min)

  • Validate 2 ideal customer personas we've pre-drafted
  • Define trigger events: what makes someone start looking?
  • Where do these people hang out — online and in Farmington?
  • How do they find agents? What makes them pick one?
  • How do probate, divorce, & commercial clients differ?
  • What words do past clients use when referring you?

Block 2 — Customer Journey Mapping (30 min)

  • Walk through each of the 7 stages together
  • Map which existing touchpoints belong where
  • Identify the gaps — where do people fall out?
  • Where is there no digital bridge between offline and online?
  • What happens after someone sees you at a Chamber event?
  • What happens after a Holly Days attendee goes home?

Block 3 — Brand Unification (15 min)

  • How do Opening Doors, Local Loop, Holly Days, CARES, magazine all connect?
  • What's the parent brand vs. sub-brands hierarchy?
  • What's the consistent thread across all touchpoints?
  • How does Annette's speaking/book brand connect to RE?
  • Trevor's face-to-face strength vs. low social interest — how to bridge?

Block 4 — Content Distribution System (15 min)

  • Build the content flow: created → clipped → distributed
  • Podcast episode → clips → social → GBP → email → magazine
  • Event content → photos → social → newsletter → GBP
  • Assign roles: Livvia, Madison Carroll, Mitchell Logic Media
  • Establish weekly/monthly cadence
  • Where does the magazine content get repurposed?
